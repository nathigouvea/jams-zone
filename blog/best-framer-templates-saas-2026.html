The 7 Best SaaS Landing Page Templates for Framer in 2026

The Framer ecosystem has grown faster than anyone expected. There are now hundreds of templates available, ranging from €29 community picks to €299 agency packs. We reviewed the top options specifically for SaaS and tech products and ranked them honestly.

If you're building a SaaS product in 2026, you already know that your landing page is often the first (and only) impression you get to make. A great template can shave days off your launch timeline and give you a professional foundation to iterate from. A bad one can cost you just as much time when you're trying to work around its limitations.

We spent time testing Framer templates specifically for SaaS use cases: subscription products, B2B tools, developer tools, and marketing software. Here's what we found.

What makes a great SaaS landing page template?

Before we get into the list, here's what we were evaluating:

  • Clear structure: Hero, features, social proof, pricing, FAQ, and CTA. Not every template gets this order right.
  • Customisability: How easy is it to change colours, fonts, copy, and layout without rebuilding from scratch?
  • Performance: Framer sites can get heavy. We looked at whether templates were bloated with animations that hurt Core Web Vitals.
  • Mobile quality: The responsive breakpoints on many templates are an afterthought. We checked each one at 375px.
  • Typography: Good hierarchy matters more than fancy effects. We assessed whether the type scale actually communicates a hierarchy.

1. Motif by Studio Motion (€79)

Motif is the most polished template on Framer's marketplace right now. It's built for B2B SaaS with a clean light-mode default, a proper pricing section with toggle, and smooth scroll animations that don't feel gimmicky. The component structure is well organised, which makes it genuinely easy to modify. The price point is higher than most, but the quality justifies it if you're launching a funded product.

Best for: B2B SaaS, productivity tools, and anything that needs to look credible fast.

2. Orbit by Luminary Studio (€49)

Orbit has a darker aesthetic that works well for developer tools and infrastructure products. It comes with a features grid, comparison table, changelog section, and a strong hero with a product mockup placeholder. The dark theme is consistent throughout — no weird light-mode patches. One downside: the FAQ accordion is a bit clunky to edit in Framer's editor.

Best for: Developer tools, API products, and technical SaaS.

3. Nova by Kris Visuals (€39)

Nova is a good mid-range option. It's lighter on features than Motif, but that's also its strength — it's faster to customise and has fewer opinions about structure. The gradient hero is beautiful and the pricing cards are clean. Not ideal if you need a lot of sections, but great for simple "here's what we do, here's the price, sign up" pages.

Best for: Simple SaaS, indie products, early-stage startups.

4. Apex by Framekit (€59)

Apex is one of the more opinionated templates on this list — it has a very specific Silicon Valley "big tech" look. Bold typographic hero, coloured feature cards, a metrics/social proof strip, and a two-tier pricing table. If your product fits that aesthetic, it's excellent. If you're building something more niche or creative, it can feel generic.

Best for: Growth-stage SaaS, AI products, anything that wants to look like a YC company.

5. Velvet by Studio Monokai (€45)

Velvet takes a more editorial approach — big serif headlines, generous whitespace, a feature-as-editorial-section layout. It's unusual for SaaS and that's actually the point. It stands out in a sea of Inter/gradient heroes. The catch: it requires more Framer experience to customise well, and the animations are heavy.

Best for: Consumer SaaS, design tools, products with a strong brand voice.

6. Base by Framer Community (Free)

The official Framer community base template is underrated. It's not glamorous, but it has a solid structure, all the key sections, and it's free. If you're in early validation mode and don't want to spend money on a template before you know the product works, Base is a sensible starting point. It will look generic without significant customisation effort, but that's the trade-off.

Best for: Prototyping, validation, and bootstrapped founders watching their spend.

7. Helix by Craftwork (€69)

Helix is for products with actual UI to show. It's built around a large product screenshot/demo section, with a clean feature list and a well-designed onboarding flow breakdown. If your SaaS has a dashboard or a clear interface, Helix makes it easy to showcase it. The animations are subtle and performance-conscious.

Best for: Any SaaS where showing the product is the main selling point.

When Framer might not be the right call

Framer is a great tool, but it's not always the right one. If you're a developer (or working with one), a well-built HTML/CSS template will outperform Framer on loading speed, SEO control, and long-term maintainability. You don't have Framer's hosting lock-in, and you can customise every line of code.
